Educational Background

My early life began in Western New York. Early on I was captured by language and began to study it more seriously as I aged. I wanted to learn as many languages as possible. My French teacher told me the best language school in the country was Middlebury College, and so I applied there for undergraduate. I graduated 2009 with a major in German and a minor in Religion. I did other significant coursework in French, Chinese, and Linguistics.

In 2010 I began to work in research administration at Tufts University. While there, I began to take courses using tuition remission and by 2015 I graduated with a Masters of Arts in Teaching with a focus on history. I did not change careers at this time, but I did change employers.

My friend asked me to apply to a job at Harvard University, and in the fall of 2015 I accepted the new position. In the course of ten years at Harvard I took many courses through their tuition remission program. Some courses were focused on linguistics, particularly historical linguistics. I also took a course on Sumerian, which was an incredible experience.
